Psychologist

My dream is to help others

Psychology has always been something that interested me. It began at a young age with a general curiosity for why people acted the way they did that would often spark conversations between my mom and I. My interest in psychology only strengthened when I realized how it could be used to help people through the form of therapy. It was then that I realized that I wanted to spend my life pursuing psychology due to my deep passion for it and the ability to utilize it to help others. Since then, most choices regarding my education and work have been made with the hope of helping me towards a future in psychology. In school I took many difficult courses to prepare myself for the worldload and study habits necessary to complete a doctorate, I took upon leadership roles in clubs and groups to learn what it meant to truly care for others and try to help them, and I was active in the drama department and Improv where I learned to be comfortable with putting myself out there and how to be outgoing to anyone I meant. In my worklife, I worked as a tutor where I learned how to effectively teach and treat people who were struggling and I currently work as a behavior technician where I help children with autism with daily living skills through the use of ABA therapy which has taught me a lot about psychology and the patience and compassion needed in the field. It’s hard to say if a person’s ready without truly knowing what’s ahead but I can easily say that I’m prepared for whatever is necessary to complete my upper division courses as well as my graduate classes. I recently read somewhere that to succeed, it is not about what will let you do something but what could stop you from succeeding. My dream to help others with therapy, change the stigma of mental health, and reform the healthcare system is strong and I believe that I can and will do it. Nothing will stop me from trying, especially not my coursework.

In the past I have worked in many fast and professional settings including resteraunts and behavioral therapy.
